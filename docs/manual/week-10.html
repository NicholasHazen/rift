Feel and legibility

Week 10Phase: ArcBudget: 5 sessionsDecisions due: lighting & moodresolution: sketch

This page sharpens when week 9's gate passes — do not elaborate it before then.

Chronicle fragment, run [unlogged] — Nothing new was built; everything got louder. A stranger harvested, built, angered the rift, and extracted without asking a single question. Award for Legibility: the HUD.

By Friday the slice will hit like a game instead of a demo — hitstop, real sound, a real HUD, a front door that connects — and a stranger will have read the whole design with zero instruction. You will understand why feel is a tuning pass here and not a construction project.

▣ Gate — definition of done

A stranger joins with no instruction and, within one session, demonstrably understands harvest, build, ire, and extract — the exam runs on the VPS with packaged clients, same as every real session since week 7. And the Friday perf gate stays green with the new UI in play.

The systems work; now strangers have to feel them. Two crafts this week, and both are tuning. Game feel first: combat reads as impactful through feedback in the ~100 ms band — a few frames of hitstop, real impact sounds, damage numbers, screen shake used like hot sauce. None of that is new construction, because the JuiceKit built in week 1 means every mechanic already carries the hooks; that bill was pre-paid, and this week collects.

Legibility second: the design runs on informed greed, and players can only choose risk they can read. Ire, instability, and haul weight must be readable at a glance by someone who has never heard the elevator pitch — and that someone arrives on Friday. Mystery in content is strategy; opacity in systems is just bad UI.

The sessions

Five sessions, sketched on purpose — the hands split and the done-whens sharpen when week 9 closes. One instruction covers all five: tune, don't add.

S1 — Hitstop & shake

Hitstop and screen shake through the JuiceKit — the hooks already sit on every mechanic, so tonight is turning dials, not writing systems. Done when: a melee hit reads as impact in the ~100 ms band without one new system written.

S2 — The SFX pass

A real sound for every verb — harvest, build, hit, kill, extract — from free CC0 packs, and the siege track from week 9 gains intro and outro states. Gerald's scream, remastered. Done when: eyes closed, you can still tell harvest from hit from kill from extraction.

S3 — The real HUD

Ire stages, the instability clock, haul weight, the session timer — UMG from C++, with benui open as the desk reference (shelf pull below). Done when: all four read at a glance mid-siege, not just in a quiet lobby.

S4 — The front door & the beacon

Menu → connect flow, plus the respawn beacon deployable. Death now has geography — where you planted it matters. Done when: a fresh packaged client reaches the rift through the menu, and dying returns you to the beacon you chose.

S5 — The legibility exam

One fresh person, zero verbal instruction, one session on the VPS. Watch. Don't ask — observe. The exam grades the HUD, not the stranger; every hesitation is a tuning note. It also opens a verdict window: do vague stages in-world hold the screen, or do the optimizers need exact numbers? → register.html#oq-05. Done when: the gate's first condition passes in front of your eyes.

⚑ Fun payload — the game starts feeling like a game

Player: the kill-confirm ding, the extraction-success fanfare, and a death screen that quotes the Chronicle's narrator in real time — "Nick has died as he lived: holding too much Goose Down." From this week that line is generated by the real templates, not authored. Builder: this whole week is the payload, and its receipt is the before/after pair — the same encounter in week-9 footage and week-10 footage, posted side by side, the most satisfying Friday Clip of the run. Both go into the reel. Where does the pair live forever? → clips.html

What this week teaches

Nothing new — that's what tuning means. Four callbacks, each a system you already own being read instead of built.

The JuiceKit collects. Week 1 wired floating text, stingers, pops, and hitstop hooks into every mechanic as it shipped, which is the only reason feel can be a one-week tuning pass. What exactly does the kit owe every mechanic? → fun-engines.html#juicekit

The HUD is a reader. Ire stages, haul weight, the clocks — every number on screen is a fact the server already replicates, so the widget owns nothing worth stealing. Why can't the client own a number that matters? → concept-netcode.html#cheater-test

The death screen is the Chronicle, live. The narrator's templates already exist; quoting one at the moment of death is a hook, not a new system. Whose voice is it, and what is it allowed to say? → fun-engines.html#chronicle

The perf gate doesn't pause for a feel week. The new HUD plays under the full week-8 load on Friday, and green stays the only acceptable color. What does the gate actually measure, and how? → concept-perf.html#gate-protocol
