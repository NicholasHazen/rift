The Reel

The manual's other pages plan; this one accumulates the record of what actually happened. Every session earns a small row: the date, a single line, and the commit range behind it. And every Friday Clip earns a highlighted slot with a caption in the Chronicle's voice — the game's deadpan campaign journal. Both kinds stack newest-first in a single stream that grows for twelve weeks, so when you want proof the slice exists beyond the plan, it's here or nowhere.

What the reel is for

This page does three jobs, and they arrive in the order they'll matter. The first is the scheduled comeback. Rewatching the week-1 waddle during the week-7 trough is a move the plan prints in advance, not something you have to invent at your lowest — two minutes of what the plumbing is for, on file and one click away. The second is deep re-entry. After a two-week gap, the save file's one-line next action tells you what to type, but the last few rows here tell you what the build has actually become — richer when you need it, skippable when you don't. The third is the long game. By week 12 this page has quietly become the trailer's shot list, for free, because every clip you already filed is a candidate cut. The plan promises; the reel shows.

The clip ritual this page archives is one of the four fun engines. Every Friday you record twenty seconds of this week's build and post it to the friends' Discord. That rule is non-negotiable, especially in the ugly weeks. Those twenty seconds of moving build each week, where friends can see it, are the project's dopamine schedule, and the reel is where that schedule compounds into a record. How to read it — and who writes it

Two kinds of row share one stream. A session row is the small kind: the date, one line distilled from that evening's tomorrow-note (the re-entry file you leave yourself), and the commit range that backs the line. That way, "what did I actually do" always has an answer a stranger could audit. A clip row is the highlighted kind: clip number, week, a Chronicle-voiced caption, and the link to the footage. Sessions number globally across the whole run, which means S001 is the project's first evening, and the save window's "sessions n/~59" counts that same series. Every row also carries a numbered anchor, like clip-001 or session-001. Those anchors are immutable once published, so any page in the manual can point at a specific Friday forever.

Nobody maintains this page by hand. Saying "close the session" appends the session row, and the clip row when there is one, in the same commit that writes the tomorrow-note and updates the save file. The mission board's save window then carries the reel's running counts. Keeping this archive alive costs no new ritual. What does the one-sentence close actually do? → doctrine-os.html#ritual

And a bad week still feeds the reel. The floor is printed law: twenty seconds of anything on screen counts, and a bug is a valid clip. What is the minimum a depleted Friday owes? → doctrine-os.html#floor
