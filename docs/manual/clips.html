The Reel

The manual's other pages plan; this one accumulates. Every session gets a row — one line and a commit range. Every Friday Clip gets an archived slot with a Chronicle-voiced caption. Newest first, one stream, growing for twelve weeks. When you want proof the slice exists beyond the plan, it's here or nowhere.

What the reel is for

Three jobs, in the order they'll matter. First, the scheduled comeback: rewatching the week-1 waddle during the week-7 trough is a printed move in the plan, not something you'll have to invent at your lowest — two minutes of what the plumbing is for, on file, one click away. Second, deep re-entry: after a two-week gap, the save file's one-line next action tells you what to type, but the last few rows here tell you what the build has become — richer when you need it, skippable when you don't. Third, the long game: by week 12 this page is the trailer shot list, for free. The plan promises; the reel shows.

The clip ritual this page archives is one of the four fun engines: twenty seconds of this week's build, recorded every Friday, posted to the friends' Discord — non-negotiable, especially in ugly weeks. Twenty seconds of moving build a week, where friends can see it, is the project's dopamine schedule, and the reel is where the schedule compounds into a record. How to read it — and who writes it

Two kinds of row share one stream. A session row is small: date, one line distilled from that evening's tomorrow-note, and the commit range that backs the line — so "what did I actually do" always has an answer a stranger could audit. A clip row is the highlighted kind: clip number, week, a Chronicle-voiced caption, and the link to the footage. Sessions number globally across the run — S001 is the project's first evening, and the save window's "sessions n/~59" counts the same series. Every row carries a numbered anchor — clip-001, session-001 — immutable once published, so any page in the manual can point at a specific Friday forever.

Nobody maintains this page by hand. Saying "close the session" appends the session row — and the clip row, when there is one — in the same commit that writes the tomorrow-note and updates the save file, and the mission board's save window carries the reel's running counts. Keeping this archive alive costs no new ritual. What does the one-sentence close actually do? → doctrine-os.html#ritual

And a bad week still feeds the reel. The floor is printed law: twenty seconds of anything on screen counts, and a bug is a valid clip. What is the minimum a depleted Friday owes? → doctrine-os.html#floor
