Two clocks

Week 09Phase: ArcBudget: 5 sessionsVerdicts due: ire decay · storm vs structuresresolution: sketch

This page sharpens when week 8's gate passes — do not elaborate it before then.

Chronicle fragment, run [unlogged] — The siege came on two fronts and the storm came on schedule. The rare nodes lit at minute fifty-one; the expedition stayed. Award for Discipline: unclaimed.

The klaxon fires mid-swing, and the horde arrives on schedule, on two fronts. Behind you the safe radius takes another step inward, and the rare nodes light up in the fringe — exactly where the air has started doing damage. The kill feed announces something with a title. The portal hums, patient. Somebody says one more rock.

By Friday you will have the full arc running — calm, escalation, siege, storm, rare-window gamble, extraction — and you will understand why the second clock turns encounters into narratives. This is the week the design's emotional engine ships.

▣ Gate — definition of done

A full session has the arc — calm → escalation → siege → storm → rare-window gamble → extraction. And in a mixed test night with friends plus bots on the VPS (the rented server box), at least one squad wipes because they got greedy. If nobody ever wipes, the dial has no teeth yet, so tune until it bites.

This week is pure design payoff. Weeks 7 and 8 bought a server anyone can join and a bot fleet that can fill it, and this week spends both on the one thing the slice exists to test. The second clock ships. Instability, the clock the world winds, joins ire, the clock the expedition winds, and once both are turning, sessions stop being encounters and become narratives — the raw material the Chronicle has been waiting for.

What ships

The siege scheduler ships a proper two-front assault, with its fronts assigned from spawn-point sets and its wave composition scaling with ire tier through the spawn tables (the data that sets each wave). Which table decides what a tier-four siege sends? → concept-ire.html#spawn-tables

The instability clock is a storm that escalates across the session, showing up as hazard zones, a tightening safe radius, and damage-over-time (a steady health drain) out at the fringes. You build it in two halves. A post-process pass (a full-screen visual effect) supplies the menace, and a GameplayEffect (the engine's unit of stat change) supplies the teeth. The shape is law. The storm is a shrinking safe radius — the legible one — while roaming hazard cells stay parked as a Stage-2 rift-modifier idea. PNW-you knows precisely what a real storm rolling in should feel like, so build that dread.

The rare window opens as instability peaks: elites spawn, each with a generated title, and rare nodes unlock. Ranged variants arrive now too, which means the melee-only law expires with the elites by design. Ranged pressure reshapes camp design, so it arrives now and not before. The best loot lives inside the storm at the exact moment leaving is smartest — the exit decision, given teeth.

The sessions

There are five sessions here, and they are sketched on purpose, because the hands split and the done-whens sharpen only once week 8 closes. The shape, though, is already firm.

S1 — The siege scheduler

This session wires timed events across two fronts, with front assignment pulled from spawn-point sets. Done when: an assault arrives on two fronts, on schedule, inside a full session.

S2 — Composition from ire tiers

[FLEET drafts, NICK reviews] Wave composition scales from ire tier through the spawn tables, and because that scaling is all JSON, the fleet drafts it while you review it like you own it. Done when: a tier change visibly changes what the next wave sends.

S3 — Instability clock & storm zones

This session builds the storm's damage-over-time GameplayEffect and its post-process pass, teeth first and menace second. That effect gets tested on Gerald first, because the ledger already knows how to hurt him. Done when: standing in the fringe drains health through the same pipeline as every other hit in the game.

S4 — The rare window

This session adds the elite archetype — more HP, one nasty ability, a generated title — plus rare nodes that stay flagged and gated by instability phase. Done when: peak instability visibly changes what's worth staying for.

S5 — Full-arc test night

This is friends plus bots on the VPS, running the whole arc end to end. Tune until at least one squad wipes from greed, and remember this gate runs live, not in PIE (the game running inside the editor). These nights also open a verdict window: does ire decay when the expedition lays low? → register.html#oq-04

What this week teaches

Nothing new is taught here, because that's what a payoff week means. You get three callbacks instead, and each one is a system you already own doing a second job.
