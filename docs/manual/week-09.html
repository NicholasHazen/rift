Two clocks

Week 09Phase: ArcBudget: 5 sessionsVerdicts due: ire decay · storm vs structuresresolution: sketch

This page sharpens when week 8's gate passes — do not elaborate it before then.

Chronicle fragment, run [unlogged] — The siege came on two fronts and the storm came on schedule. The rare nodes lit at minute fifty-one; the expedition stayed. Award for Discipline: unclaimed.

The klaxon fires mid-swing, and the horde arrives on schedule, on two fronts. Behind you the safe radius takes another step inward, and the rare nodes light up in the fringe — exactly where the air has started doing damage. The kill feed announces something with a title. The portal hums, patient. Somebody says one more rock.

By Friday you will have the full arc running — calm, escalation, siege, storm, rare-window gamble, extraction — and you will understand why the second clock turns encounters into narratives. This is the week the design's emotional engine ships.

▣ Gate — definition of done

A full session has the arc — calm → escalation → siege → storm → rare-window gamble → extraction — and in a mixed test night (friends plus bots, on the VPS), at least one squad wipes because they got greedy. If nobody ever wipes, the dial has no teeth yet; tune until it bites.

Pure design payoff. Weeks 7 and 8 bought a server anyone can join and a bot fleet that can fill it; this week spends both on the thing the slice exists to test. The second clock ships: instability, the clock the world winds, joins ire, the clock the expedition winds — and sessions stop being encounters and become narratives, the raw material the Chronicle has been waiting for.

What ships

The siege scheduler: a proper two-front assault, fronts assigned from spawn-point sets, wave composition scaling with ire tier through the spawn tables. Which table decides what a tier-four siege sends? → concept-ire.html#spawn-tables

The instability clock: a storm escalating across the session — hazard zones, a tightening safe radius, damage-over-time at the fringes; post-process for menace, a GameplayEffect for teeth. The shape is law: the storm is a shrinking safe radius — the legible one — and roaming hazard cells stay parked as a Stage-2 rift-modifier idea. PNW-you knows precisely what a real storm rolling in should feel like — build that dread.

The rare window: as instability peaks, elites spawn — with generated titles — and rare nodes unlock. Ranged variants arrive now too: the melee-only law expires with the elites by design — ranged pressure reshapes camp design, so it arrives now and not before. The best loot lives inside the storm at the exact moment leaving is smartest — the exit decision, given teeth.

The sessions

Five sessions, sketched on purpose — the hands split and the done-whens sharpen when week 8 closes. The shape is already firm.

S1 — The siege scheduler

Timed events, two fronts, front assignment from spawn-point sets. Done when: an assault arrives on two fronts, on schedule, inside a full session.

S2 — Composition from ire tiers

[FLEET drafts, NICK reviews] Wave composition scales from ire tier through the spawn tables — all JSON, which is exactly why the fleet drafts it and you review it like you own it. Done when: a tier change visibly changes what the next wave sends.

S3 — Instability clock & storm zones

The GameplayEffect DoT and the post-process pass — teeth first, menace second. The storm DoT gets tested on Gerald first; the ledger already knows how to hurt him. Done when: standing in the fringe drains health through the same pipeline as every other hit in the game.

S4 — The rare window

The elite archetype — more HP, one nasty ability, a generated title — plus rare nodes flagged and gated by instability phase. Done when: peak instability visibly changes what's worth staying for.

S5 — Full-arc test night

Friends plus bots on the VPS, the whole arc end to end. Tune until at least one squad wipes from greed — the gate runs live, not in PIE. These nights also open a verdict window: does ire decay when the expedition lays low? → register.html#oq-04
