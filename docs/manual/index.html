The route

Week 0 is the rig: engine, repo, fleet, and a codename ceremony, boxed tight so setup can't become a residence. Then twelve weeks in five phases. Core loop, weeks 1–4: a node you whack, chunks you carry, combat through GAS, a horde that answers, and the ire system that turns greed into violence. Camp, weeks 5–6: the camp becomes a machine, and the loop closes into one whole story — drop, harvest, extract, Chronicle. Steel, weeks 7–8: real infrastructure, then the performance work that earns 24 players. Arc, weeks 9–10: the two clocks tuned against each other, then feel and legibility. Test, weeks 11–12: strangers play, and the hypothesis gets its verdict. What exactly is the hypothesis, and what counts as passing? → the-slice.html.

Four ember markers ride the top of the map — the fun engines coming online: the Friday clip at week 0, the JuiceKit at week 1, the Director's Remote at week 4, the Chronicle at week 6. Two markers sit below the line: the week-8 perf gate, which the world grades, and the weeks-11–12 fun test, which strangers do.

Two clocks, three colors

Color is meaning here, and the meaning comes from your own design: ember marks the player-driven clock — builds, fun payloads, things you push. storm violet marks the world's clock — concepts, systems, open questions the world will answer. moss marks gates: the definition-of-done that decides whether a week is finished. The manual is painted in the same two-clock scheme as the game. That's not decoration; it's rehearsal.

How this manual works

The manual changes through four moves, and only four. A content edit lands on the one page that owns the fact and logs a row in the changelog — every explanation in this wiki has exactly one home, and every other mention of it is a link. When reality answers an open question, the answer moves up into the register as a dated decision; the question is deleted where it stood, and the decided design folds into its owning page as plain law, no scar tissue. A Friday gate pass flips a glyph in the tracker above, appends a window to the archive, and sharpens the next week's page — only the next one, because pretending to have week-11 answers in week 0 is how plans calcify. And a session close writes the first concrete action of the next session into docs/tomorrow-note.md and updates the save file, so the front door never lies about where you stand.

That's the whole machine. Weeks 7 through 12 are deliberately sketches; they sharpen one gate at a time, fed by what the finished weeks actually taught. The agent-facing contract — voice, style, review checklists, the four transactions in full — lives at _meta/style-guide.md; this section is the human summary, not a second copy. The manual should be smarter in week 6 than it is today.

A closing note from your mentor

Twelve weeks from now the most likely outcome is not triumph or disaster — it's a build that's 70% of this manual, two weeks behind the calendar, with a greed dial that's almost fun, a folder of Friday Clips, and one playtest quote you can't stop thinking about. That is a wildly successful outcome. It means the hypothesis is answerable, and you built the machine that answers it — and if the Fun Mandate held, you're still smiling at the kill feed in week 9, which is the hidden objective this whole manual actually optimizes for.

The failure modes all share one root: substituting input for output. Watching instead of building. Reading engine source instead of shipping the week. Browsing Fab instead of tuning spawn tables. When you feel the drift, the fix is always the same and always available: open the tomorrow-note, do the first concrete action, and let momentum handle the rest.

You're bringing unusual advantages — fifteen years of systems instinct, an agent fleet, a design deliberately shaped for a team of one, and a doctrine that refuses to let the work go joyless. What these twelve weeks buy is the one thing none of that can shortcut: judgment about what's fun, formed by watching real people play a real thing you really built — and laughing at the Chronicle afterward, together.

Go rig the workshop. The rift's waiting, and so is Gerald.
