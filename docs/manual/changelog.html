Changelog

Every other page in this manual is forbidden a past. This one is the past: one row per change, append-only, newest at the bottom — the only page where history is allowed to accumulate.

The whole wiki is held to the rebase test: any page, read cold, should sound like it was written in one sitting yesterday. That trick only works if the sittings are counted somewhere, and this is the somewhere. When the plan changes, the owning page states the new law in plain present tense and moves on; the fact that anything changed lands here as a row. You never need this page at 9pm. It exists for the fleet, for audits, and for the one legitimate history question: when did that change, and what did it touch?

What earns a row

A row is rev, date, change, pages touched — one sentence in the change cell, no essays. Five events earn one:

  • A content edit. What changed and why, one line, every page it touched named.
  • A question resolution. The question leaves the open list and becomes a dated present-tense decision in the register; the row records the move and links the register anchor. Decision content never lives here — this page says that, the register says what. Where do decisions and open questions live? → register.html
  • A gate pass. The Friday row carries the one honest sentence: what shipped, what slipped, what surprised. How does the full Friday close work? → doctrine-os.html#friday
  • A physics adjudication. Playtests decide what each Tier-2 stand-in becomes; every graduate-or-retire verdict logs here, so the doctrine page stays clean law. Which stand-ins are on trial, and what graduates them? → doctrine-physics.html
  • A new page. The wiki is capped at 34 files; a new one buys its way in with a row justifying its existence.

Rows are cheap; write them at commit time, not from memory. One row per change, one honest sentence per gate.
