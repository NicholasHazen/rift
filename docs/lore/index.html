What this is

The companion book to the Stage 1 slice. It is written in lore-writer mode — Claude writes the prose, Nick owns the world — and it exists for one reason: so the builder has a story to inhabit while the systems come up. Every chapter dramatizes the mechanic its build-week lands, which means the book and the game verify each other: if a chapter can't make a system feel like something a crew would tell stories about, that's design intelligence, not just fiction trouble.

The world bible holds everything the book treats as true, with every invention tagged provisional until Nick vetoes or blesses it. The one rule that never bends: the game's manual is senior canon, and the fiction follows it — never the reverse.

The reading contract

One chapter per build week, released as the week opens — the book deliberately does not run ahead of the build. Reading chapter VI before week 6 would spend the door's mystery early; arriving at each system with its story fresh is the whole motivational design. The prologue is the exception: it is live now, before week 0, because it is the legend every later chapter assumes you know.
